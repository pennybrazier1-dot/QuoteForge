# QuoteForge Proposal Template

## Purpose

Every QuoteForge proposal should be:

- Clear
- Professional
- Easy for the customer to understand
- Easy for the tradesperson to edit
- Written in plain English
- Honest about what is and is not included

AI assists in producing the first draft, but the tradesperson is always responsible for reviewing the proposal before sending it.

---

# Proposal Structure

## 1. Customer Details

Include:

- Customer name
- Property address
- Proposal number
- Proposal date
- Valid until

---

## 2. Job Summary

A short paragraph explaining what the customer has requested.

Example:

> Replace the existing timber fence with concrete posts, gravel boards and new fence panels. Remove the old fencing and dispose of all waste.

---

## 3. Scope of Work

List exactly what is included.

Example:

- Remove existing fence
- Supply new materials
- Install concrete posts
- Install gravel boards
- Install fence panels
- Remove waste from site

---

## 4. Materials

Clearly list the main materials that will be supplied.

---

## 5. Labour

Explain the work to be carried out and, where possible, provide an estimated duration.

---

## 6. Estimated Duration

State the expected timescale.

Example:

> Approximately two working days.

---

## 7. Things to Confirm

Highlight any assumptions or information that should be confirmed before work begins.

Example:

- Access to the rear garden
- Parking availability
- Final fence height
- Hidden ground conditions

---

## 8. Optional Extras

Clearly separate optional work from the quoted work.

Optional items should never be included in the total unless accepted.

---

## 9. Payment Terms

Use the business's default payment terms unless changed by the tradesperson.

---

## 10. Proposal Validity

State how long the quotation remains valid.

Default:

14 days

---

## 11. Acceptance

Explain that the customer accepts the proposal by approving it through QuoteForge or by another agreed method.

---

# Writing Guidelines

Every proposal should:

- Be friendly but professional.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Avoid exaggerated sales language.
- Explain technical work in simple terms where possible.
- Never invent materials or measurements.
- Never promise work that has not been requested.
- Clearly identify assumptions.
- Keep sentences concise.
- Use bullet points where they improve readability.

---

# QuoteForge Principle

The proposal should help both the tradesperson and the customer understand exactly what work is being offered.

If anything is uncertain, it should be listed under **Things to Confirm**, not guessed.
